New Delhi

Delhi, India’s capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the country’s north. In Old Delhi, a neighborhood dating to the 1600s, stands the imposing Mughal-era Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque, whose courtyard accommodates 25,000 people. Nearby is Chandni Chowk, a vibrant bazaar filled with food carts, sweets shops and spice stalls.

Delhi is the capital city of India. It is also the political hub of India. Delhi is a union territory and is officially called the National Capital Territory (NCT) of Delhi. Delhi is located in the northern part of India. It is surrounded by Haryana on all sides except the east, where it borders with Uttar Pradesh. Delhi is famous for its rich culture and history, authentic food, and fashion. It is also popular with tourists from around the world. Delhi was made a Union Territory in 1956. It is one of eight union territories in India. The other union territories.
